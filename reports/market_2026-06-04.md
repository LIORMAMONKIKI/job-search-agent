## 🇮🇱 Israel AI Hiring Landscape — June 2026

---

### 📈 What's Growing / Shrinking

**Growing:**



Israel's high-tech sector is entering 2026 with renewed confidence, steady hiring, and a widening salary gap favoring AI specialists — and despite global uncertainty, the local tech market has maintained stability while continuing to grow.





According to a comprehensive employer survey by the Israel Innovation Authority, roughly **42% of Israeli high-tech companies** now employ dedicated AI roles, from researchers to GenAI engineers.





Israel's GenAI startup count now stands at **342 companies** actively building GenAI products. A notable trend is the shift from standalone generative tools to **agentic AI systems** — over half of the new companies added to the landscape (104 out of 198) claim to offer agentic capabilities.

 ([Remagine Ventures / Calcalist](https://www.calcalistech.com/ctechnews/article/s1yl6mlulx))



AI is now nearly universal in Israel's high-tech sector, with **95% of employees using AI tools regularly** and 78% using them daily, according to a new nationwide survey of more than 500 tech workers by the Israel Innovation Authority and the Brookdale Institute.



**Shrinking:**



Layoffs, fewer junior hires, companies recruiting abroad, and longer hiring processes paint a concerning picture — but a deeper look reveals the market is not shrinking; it is undergoing transformation.

 ([Ynetnews / Aman Group report](https://www.ynetnews.com/tech-and-digital/article/skofzljxze), June 2026)



Juniors are often the first to feel pressure as AI tools boost senior productivity, potentially reducing demand for entry-level tasks.

 

Companies show a clear preference for **senior candidates with at least five years of experience**, particularly those with deep mastery of AI tools.





The average hiring cycle for a technology position has increased from roughly **30 days two years ago to between 45 and 60 days** today

 — a sign of greater scrutiny per hire rather than volume hiring.
