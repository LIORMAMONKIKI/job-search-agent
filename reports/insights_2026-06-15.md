### Which Bucket Favours Lior — and Why

**International, clearly.**

Three compounding reasons:

1. **Title surface is wider.** "Generative AI Associate" (37), "Creative Technologist" (7), "Technical Artist" (7) are roles written *for* the profile Lior is building — creative execution + AI tooling fluency. Israel's equivalent titles total ~26 roles across a smaller pool with higher analytics baseline requirements.

2. **Lior's tooling is a rare signal internationally, not just domestically.** ComfyUI (26 demand), ElevenLabs (18 demand), prompt engineering (106 demand) in the international pool are skills where Lior has concrete, demonstrable depth. In Israel's pool, these same skills appear at 8, not listed, and 24 — a fraction of the signal weight.

3. **Brand-first filtering plays to Lior's strengths.** International roles filter heavily on portfolio visibility and perceived creative authority. Lior's ability to produce a strong public-facing body of work (showreel, GitHub, case studies) converts directly into application leverage. Israel's relocation-first filter is harder to hack from the outside.

**Israel is not a dead end** — the 11 High-match roles there are an immediate hit list worth pursuing in parallel, and Technical Artist (5) + Prompt Engineer (5) are low-competition niches with strong Lior fit. But as the *primary* market, it's the wrong bet.
